Sylvester-Gallai Theorem

The Sylvester-Gallai Theorem is a deceptively simple theorem in geometry. It meets all four of my criteria for a great theorem: Easy to state Interesting to think about Hard to prove from scratch Has a simple proof The theorem is as follows: For any finite set of distinct points in the Euclidean plane, either all of the points lie on a single line; or there exists a line that contains exactly two of the points. Three Worlds Collide

I read a very thought provoking story: Three Worlds Collide, by Eliezer Yudkowsky. One high level moral question that the story asks, is how to treat an alien species with severely different moral viewpoints from one’s own. In particular, how to act when the other species’ actions are horrific, by one’s own moral stance. Call this the “horrific morals” question. Here I will not attempt to tackle the more difficult object-level moral question of which of the three species moral’s are good or horrific, but instead the meta-level moral question of how one should act upon encountering such a species.
